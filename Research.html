Research Experiences

Projects

• Saliency Detection in 3D video based on GANs (Graduation Thesis)

Generate Adversarial Networks (GANs) have a very good potential to handle the end-to-end problems and saliency detection is exactly one of them. Thus this project aims at upgrading the traditional method with deep learning way to segment the saliency area from a video sequence with depth. This is also the topic of my graduation thesis, supervised by Prof. Ping Zhang.

• Saliency Detection in 3D video [DRAFTS to ICME]

Saliency or visual attention is a significant mechanism both in human cognition and computational science. In this project, we aim to extract the saliency object from 3D video sequence. By designing a multi-scale approach with SLIC algorithm we can decompose the whole image into none-overlapping superpixels with a well preserved edge and shape. A graphical model is also utilized to refine the initial saliency map. Our proposal is general and robust as well as unsupervised. Our work has been submitted to ICME2018.

• Infrared Weak Target Detection based on deep learning

Infrared image is often used to detect the object like the flight (generate lots of heat) in the sky and the object in the image is often small and with a weak feature. In order to recognize it accurately and distinguish it from the cloud in the sky, a Convolutional Neural Network is adapted to recognize and classify the object in the picture. With the high-efficiency CNN architecture, the result is fantastic. A patent has been accepted in this project.
